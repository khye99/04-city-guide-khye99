    Top 5 Places to Visit in Florence

    Welcome To Florence!

    Florence, the spectacular capital of the region of Tuscany, is located on Italy's northwest coast. It is a stunning medieval city divided into two parts by the River Arno that flows from the Apennines through Florence and Pisa. Florence is considered one of the most beautiful cities in the world with its overflow of palaces, churches, and museums filled with masterful paintings and sculptures. What was once the cradle of the Renaissance, Florence gives its people beauty, history, and culture. Here are the top 5 places you should visit when planning a trip to Florence.

    1. The Florence Cathedral

    The Cattedrale di Santa Maria del Fiore is the main church of Florence, Italy. The Duomo, as it is ordinarily called, was begun in 1296 in the Gothic style with the design of Arnolfo di Cambio and compleded structurally almost 150 years later in 1436. The Duomo is considered to be one of the world's greatest churches and is the top tourist destination in Florence.

    2. Uffizi Gallery & Galleria dell'Accademia

    The wonders of this city are contained, as in a treasure chest, in its fabulous museums: each museum is a window on the Italian art, history and culture, an endless source of knowledge and beauty! The Uffizi Gallery & Galleria dell'Accademia are two of Florence's most prominent destinations to enrich yourself with something truly special. The Uffizi Gallery (or Galleria degli Uffizi, in Italian) is the most famous museum in Florence, and one of the most famous and important museums in the world. The Accademia Gallery in Florence is also called the Museum of Michelangelo. Created by the Grand Duke Pietro Leopoldo in 1784 as a place of study for students of the nearby Academy of Fine Arts (Accademia di Belle Arti) which was the first academy of drawning in Europe, the Accademy Gallery (Galleria dell'Accademia) in Florence today retains some of the most beautiful works of Michelangelo, such as the famous "David".

    4. San Lorenzo Market

    Whether you are looking for an activity for the whole family, a Tuscan meal, hunting for those local ingredients to try your hands at a Florentine dish or for a souvenir to bring home, the San Lorenzo Market is the place for you. The market is actually comprised of two separate markets, an indoor market, known as the Mercato Centrale, or Central Market, is home to all things food. The outdoor section that lines the surroundings streets of the large Mercato Centrale building, sells leather, clothing, and many other souvenirs. Both the outdoor and indoor markets are a great addition to your sightseeing and to the museum visits you have on your itinerary (especially if you need a break from museums in Florence) so make sure to set a whole morning aside to explore the market, and shop until your heart's content.
    5. Ponte Vecchio
    The Ponte Vecchio is the oldest bridge in Florence. It is believed that a bridge already existed here during the Roman times. Its current appearance dates back to 1345 when it was built to replace a bridge which was destroyed by a flood. Houses were built on the bridge, a common practice in large European cities during the Middle Ages. This pedestrian bridge is often teeming with tourists and the many musicians, portraitists, and other entertainers who create a constantly vibrant atmosphere.
